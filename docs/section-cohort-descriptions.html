2.2.1 Bangladesh

The Bangladesh study was a impact evaluation study of 1862 children around the age of 18 months. The Bayley Scale for Infant and Todler Development-II (Bayley-II) was administered and long-term follow-up data was available for the Wechsler Preschool and Primary Scale of Intelligence (WPPSI) at 5 years.

2.2.2 Brazil 1

The Brazil 1 study was a birth-cohort of 3 assements: 644 children at 3 months, 1412 children at 6 months and 1362 children at 12 months. In each round the Denver-II was administered. Long term follow-up data was available for the Wechsler Adult Intelligence Scale (WAIS) at 18 years.

2.2.3 Brazil 2

The Brazil 2 study was a birth-cohort with measurements of 3907 children at 12 months and 3869 children at 24 months. On both occasions the Battelle Development Inventory (BDI-2) was administered.

2.2.4 Chile 1

The Chile 1 study was an impact evaluation study of 128 children assessed at 6 months, 1732 children at 12 months and 279 at 18 months. Each wave the Bayley-I was administered. Long-term follow-up data were available for the WPPSI at 5-6 years.

2.2.5 Chile 2

The Chile 2 study was a brith-cohort of 4869 children who were administered BDI-2 at 7-23 months and 9201 children who were administered the Test de Desarrollo Psicomotor (Tepsi) at 24-58 months. For the latter group, follow-up data were available for Peabody Picture Vocabulary Test (PPVT) at 5-6 years.

2.2.6 China

The China study was an impact evaluation study that contained 990 children assessed with the Bayley-III at 18 months.

2.2.7 Colombia 1

The Colombia 1 study was an impact evaluation study that has two waves, wave 1 contained 704 children at 12-24 motnhs and wave 2 631 at 24-41 months. Each time the Bayley-III was administered. Long-term follow-up data were available for PPVT at 4-6 years.

2.2.8 Colombia 2

The colombia 2 study was an instrument validation study where all 1311 children aged 6-42 months were adminstered the Bayley-II. For a subgroup of 658 children the Denver Developmental Screening Test (Denver-II), the Ages and Stages Questionnaire (ASQ-3) and the BDI-2 Screener were administered as well. Long-term follow-up data were available for teh Wechsler Intelligence Scale for Children (WISC-V) and the PPVT.

2.2.9 Ecuador

In Ecuador and impact evaluation study was performed for 667 childen between 0-35 months where the Barrera Moncada (Barrera) was administered. Long-term follow-up data was available for the PPVT at 5-8 and 9-12 years.

2.2.10 Ethiopia

The Ethiopia study was a birth-cohort with 193 children of 12 months in the first wave, 440 children of 30 motnhs in the second wave anv 456 children of 42 months in the third wave. Each wave the Bayley-III was administered. Long-term follow-up data were available for the PPVT at 10-11 years.

2.2.11 Jamaica 1

The Jamaica 1 study was an impact evaluation study were the Griffiths Mental Development Scales (Griffiths) was administed to 225 children at 15 months in the first wave, and 218 children at 24 months in the second wave. Long-term follow-up data were available for WPPSI and PPVT at 6 years.

2.2.12 Jamaica 2

The Jamaica 2 study was an impact evaluation study were the Griffiths was administed to 159 children at 9-24 months, 21-36 months ant at 33-48 months. Long-term follow-up data were available for Stanford Binet Intelligence Scales (SB-5), Ravenâ€™s Coloured Progressive Matrices (Ravens), and PPVT at 7-8 years and the WAIS at 17-18 years.

2.2.13 Madagascar

The Madagascar study was an impact evaluation study where the SB-5 was administered to 205 children ages 34-42 months. Long-term follow-up data was available for SB-5 and PPVT at 7-11 years.

2.2.14 Netherlands 1

The Netherlands 1 study was an instrument validation study wit a total of 9 waves. Each wave the Dutch Developmental Inventory (DDI) was administered. The first wave included 1985 children at 1 months, wave 2 1807 childen at 2 months, wave 3 1963 children at 3 months, wave 4 1919 children at 6 months, wave 5 1881 children at 9 months, wave 6 1802 children at 12 months, wave 7 1776 children at 15 months, wave 8 1787 childrent at 18 months, and wave 9 1815 children at 24 months.

2.2.15 Netherlands 2

The Netherlands 2 study was an instrument validation study with a total of 5 waves, Each wave the DDI was administered. Wave 1 included 1016 children at 24 months, wave 2 995 children at 30 monts, wave 3, 1592 children at 36 months, wave 4 1592 children at 42 months, and wave 5 1024 childen at 48 months.

2.2.16 South Africa

The South Africa study was a birth cohort with four waves. The first wave inlcuded 485 children and seccond wave 275 children, who were assessed at 6 and 12 months, respectively, with the Bayley-I and the Griffiths. The third wave included 1802 children and the fourth wave 1614 children, assessed at 24 and 48 months, respectively, with the Vineland Social Maturity Scale (Vineland).
